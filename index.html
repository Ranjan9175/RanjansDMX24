HYDRO BLASTERS

Introduction:

Do you have an interest in aerodynamic designing!? Also, do you love flying rockets? If your answer is yes, we will provide you with an excellent opportunity to build your rocket and fly it yourself. This is a competitive event, where you would need to design a bottle rocket that would fly the farthest. The rocket with an excellent aerodynamic design and a proper launching technique would bag a huge prize. So, rocket enthusiasts turn on your engines and get ready to launch!.
Rules:
• Rockets should be made on the spot within the time limit(45 mins)Before the 1st round.
• Only cardboard should be used for wings.
• A single 2 litre water bottle can be used.
• The only allowed source of propulsion will be water and air pressure.
• The pressure must not exceed 60 psi.
• Metal, glass, hard plastics, spikes and any kind of antennas must not be utilized to construct your rocket.
• The usage of parachutes is strictly prohibited.


Format of Rounds:
1. Bottle Rocket Building- Each team must build their own bottle rocket on the spot utilizing their own resources in the time permitted. Teams capable of completing the rockets within the time restriction will be chosen. The finished rocket is first inspected and it is allowed to be launched if its considered eligible and safe by the judges. The team is provided with one trial launch (optional) and one main launch which will be considered for the points. Rules and restriction for material usage will be announced in detail before starting of the registration process for the tournament.
2. Range test - Each team is given 2 chances of launching to reach a specified distance from the launch pad. If the target is not reached, no points are awarded. The farther the distance greater the points.
3. Accuracy test - Launch the rocket and land the target zone. Two launches are allowed per team Points would be given upon the landing area and the accuracy. All teams will be participating in all three rounds. If the rocket fails and is broken in action then it cannot proceed to next round. Judging Criteria: The trajectory of the rocket and its stability are judged and points are awarded suitably.
